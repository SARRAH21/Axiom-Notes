Real Analysis Essentials — §2.3
Theorem 2.3.1 (Mean Value Theorem)
Let \(f : [a,b] \to \mathbb{R}\) be continuous on \([a,b]\) and differentiable on \((a,b)\). Then there exists \(c \in (a,b)\) such that
\[ f'(c) = \frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a} \]
Proof
Define \(g(x) = f(x) - \frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}(x-a)\). Then \(g\) is continuous on \([a,b]\), differentiable on \((a,b)\), and \(g(a)=g(b)=f(a)\). By Rolle's Theorem, there exists \(c \in (a,b)\) with \(g'(c)=0\), giving the result. □
Example
Let \(f(x)=x^2\) on \([1,3]\). Then \(\frac{f(3)-f(1)}{3-1}=\frac{8}{2}=4\). Since \(f'(x)=2x\), we find \(c=2 \in (1,3)\). ✓
